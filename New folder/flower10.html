Origin of Roses

Roses primarily originate from Asia, with additional species native to Europe, North America, and Northwest Africa. They have been cultivated for over 5,000 years, especially in China, Persia, and India. Their extensive cultivation has made them a beloved and versatile flower worldwide.

Origin of Stock Flowers

Stock flowers, scientifically known as Matthiola incana, are native to the Mediterranean region and the Canary Islands. They have been cultivated for centuries in Europe for their fragrant and colorful blooms. Stock flowers are popular in floral arrangements for their sweet scent and variety of colors.

Origin of Wax Flowers

Wax flowers, known botanically as Chamelaucium, originate from Western Australia. They thrive in the region's dry, Mediterranean climate and are celebrated for their small, waxy blooms and long vase life. These flowers are commonly used in floral arrangements and are valued for their delicate appearance and hardiness.
